Syphilis: an overlooked outbreak

Syphilis, a sexually transmitted disease caused by the bacterium Treponema pallidum, is often seen as outdated an outdated sexually transmitted disesase, easily cured by medicine. However, the United States has seen a rise of the STD in the past 15 years.

The Center for Disease Control released that there were 19,999 cases of primary and secondary syphilis reported in 2014, a 15.1 percent increase since 2013. That means 6.3 out of every 100,000 people in the United States contracted primary or secondary syphilis in 2014.

An increased risk for gay and bisexual communities

The Center for Disease Control said in their 2014 National Data for Chlamydia, Gonorrhea, and Syphilis report that "syphilis infection can also place a person at increased risk for acquiring or transmitting HIV infection. Available surveillance data indicate that an average of half of MSM (men who have sex with men) who have syphilis are also infected with HIV."
